# Reviewer Attacks

Attack: A tuned threshold solved the old split.

Response: The final paper agrees and uses this as a negative control. The new evidence is the shifted full-scale benchmark.

Attack: The tuned phase threshold has higher F1.

Response: It has 0.717 F1 versus 0.711 for the boundary field. The boundary field wins on utility, success, unsafe ceding, missed reclaim, chatter, peak force, and recovery latency.

Attack: The results are simulated.

Response: The paper is framed as deterministic mechanism evidence and does not claim hardware safety validation.

Attack: The boundary field is hand-designed.

Response: The coefficients are not the deployable contribution. The contribution is the boundary representation, the shift stress test, and the demonstrated safety-stability tradeoff.
