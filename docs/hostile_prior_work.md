## Why they are hostile
- They already optimize assistive control under human input.
- They often include context, switching, or adaptation.
- They can force the thesis to prove that a boundary model is not just another name for arbitration.

## Notes
- The strongest hostile comparison is likely the intent-inference + arbitration line.
- The second strongest is automatic mode switching under assistive teleoperation.
- For the final paper, those need explicit side-by-side failure cases where confidence is high but the physical context still requires ceding authority, or vice versa.
